Every reel on this page is a single, unedited pass through the property — the gate at dawn, the durbar at rest, a suite before the linens are turned. We built this site as we would guide a first-time guest: in order, unhurried, chapter by chapter.

The Journey

Arrival to nightfall, in the order you'll live it

02

The Arrival Gate

Hand-riveted teak, iron studs cast in the palace's own foundry, and a threshold that has not moved in a hundred and sixty years. The car stops. The gate does the rest.

03

The Durbar Lobby

Once the hall of public audience, now the first room a guest stands in. The frescoes are original; the silence is by design — no piped music competes with forty-foot ceilings.

04

The Royal Suite

Six hundred square feet that once housed a Maharaja's private study. The desk is still where he left it. The Wi-Fi, mercifully, is not from the same era.

05

The Pool & Gardens

A stepwell reimagined as a lap pool, bordered by champa trees planted for a wedding in 1932. Breakfast is served at the waterline, whenever you decide morning begins.

06

The Evening, Lit

Four thousand oil lamps, placed by hand along every parapet, every solstice. This is the last thing our guests see before dinner — and the first thing they ask us to explain.
